THE PROBLEM

Infrastructure mobilisation is decided on incomplete information — every morning.

Constrained operations — tidal infrastructure, confined access, complex sequencing, safety-critical permits — require dozens of conditions to align before a crew can move. Wind thresholds. Tidal windows. Permit validity. Contractor readiness. Equipment clearance.

That information sits across multiple systems, documents, and inboxes. Nobody connects it the night before. The decision to mobilise is made on instinct, experience, and incomplete data — at 7am, when the crew is already at the gate.

The cost is not just financial. It is programme time, crew confidence, and the compounding effect of repeated, preventable delays.

WHAT THIS LOOKS LIKE IN PRACTICE

Real incidents. Preventable decisions.

A dive team mobilised to a tidal infrastructure site. The tidal window was known. The weather forecast was available. A confined space permit had been issued. A crane was on standby.

What nobody had connected, the night before, was that the crane's wind threshold had been exceeded in the overnight forecast — and the confined space permit had been issued for a different access point than the one planned for that morning. The crew arrived. The work could not proceed.

Three hours of standby pay. A compressed tidal window lost. The sequence pushed back two days.

"Nobody did anything wrong. The system simply failed to connect the dots the night before."

This is not a rare failure mode. It is the default operating condition for constrained infrastructure works — and it compounds across every programme that runs without a pre-mobilisation readiness check.

HOW IT WORKS

The night before is when the decision gets made.

01

Reads the constraint stack

Permits, method statements, risk assessments, programme documents. Slackwater reads what's in the system and extracts what matters — expiry dates, scope conditions, personnel requirements, plant restrictions.

02

Checks live conditions

Weather forecast against wind and tidal thresholds. Programme schedule against planned task sequence. Personnel clearances against operational requirements.

03

Produces the brief

One document. What can move tomorrow, what cannot, and why. Named constraints. Specific conditions. Delivered before the crews leave home.

04

Builds the dataset

Every brief adds to a permanent record of decisions, conditions, and outcomes. Over time, that dataset becomes the operational intelligence asset — the pattern of how a site actually behaves.
