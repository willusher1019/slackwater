THE PROBLEM

Infrastructure programmes lose millions before 8am.

Permits not confirmed Crews arrive before access documents are in place. Work cannot begin.
Environmental thresholds unmet Wind, tide, or water conditions fall outside operational limits discovered only at site.
Third-party works unresolved Adjacent operations, inspections, or contractor conflicts are not reconciled the night before.
Crew readiness not established Minimum complement not met. Specialist roles absent. Work stood down after mobilisation.
Access windows misread Tidal, possession, or airside windows are narrower than planned. Productive time is lost.
Sequencing not assessed Predecessor tasks incomplete. The work that was supposed to happen next cannot start.
WHAT THIS LOOKS LIKE IN PRACTICE
INCIDENT 01 · THAMES BARRIER · MAY 2026
£12,400
Crew stood down — minimum complement not met
13 DiveCo personnel mobilised to Leaf Golf for primary anode cutting works. One unplanned absentee meant minimum crew for arm access was not met. Primary task stood down for the day. Personnel redeployed to secondary cleaning tasks. Productive arm time: zero.
WHAT SLACKWATER WOULD HAVE DONE
Crew confirmation check at 19:00 the night before would have identified the shortfall. Stand-down issued before mobilisation. Resequencing planned overnight.
INCIDENT 02 · THAMES BARRIER · MAY 2026
£8,200
Works aborted — unscheduled third-party inspection
Jet washing operations on main passage Golf were aborted at 15:00 when an unscheduled Rendel Ltd inspection arrived on site. The inspection had not been communicated at morning briefing. Approximately 30 minutes of productive time lost. Environment Agency crane conflict added a further 6-minute setup delay.
WHAT SLACKWATER WOULD HAVE DONE
Third-party diary reconciliation the night before would have flagged the Rendel inspection. Works sequence adjusted. Crew briefed before arrival.
